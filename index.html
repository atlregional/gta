How is transit used in your district?

Transit in Georgia includes rail, buses, vans, meeting the needs of hundreds of thousands of Georgians every day. In alone, transit operators provide 1,000,000 trips annually. These services are especially important to the 500,000 elderly and 200,000 disabled riders that depend on transit in this district.

How much does transit cost in your district?

Transit expenses are divided into two types capital and operating. Capital expenses include infrastructure and equipment, such as buses and vans (or "rolling stock"), facilities, and technology. Operating expenses include the day-to-day activities required to operate the system, such as salaries for bus operators and mechanics, regular vehicle and system maintenance, and general administrative costs. Meeting operating expenses has traditionally been one of the most significant challenges facing transit systems in Georgia.
